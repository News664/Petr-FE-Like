# Inevitable Eternity — Defeat Scenes: Act 3

> Act 3 — Ch10, Ch11, Ch12, Ch13.
> Recovery window now exists — killing the Collector before it reaches a petrified unit
> prevents capture. Some characters avoid capture this way. Those who don't are handled below.

---

## Ch10 — The Meeting That Wasn't

---

### Mid-Chapter

*The convergence point had been a known location — both groups had planned to meet here. The enemy knew it too.*

*The NPCs who had gathered at the meeting point for other reasons — a grain merchant, a factor with his ledgers, a scout from a third party who had been watching the road — were frozen at their tables and posts before the player groups arrived. The meeting point was occupied, but not by anyone who could speak.*

*The Collector units moved through the perimeter. Each one walked to a newly petrified player unit — not running, unhurried — and touched the stone. The touch transferred the capture status. The Collector moved to the next.*

*This was the first chapter where the player could kill the Collector before it reached a frozen ally. A few would survive this way. Most wouldn't — the Collectors were fast and numerous, and the player group had just been split for the approach.*

---

### Tana

*A Standard Gorgon stepped around the corner and caught her mid-step — the same forward stride she'd been making since Ch1, the same weight-forward posture. An officer nearby said, not even glancing at her: "East gallery. The same stride, same direction."*

*She felt it before the stone moved. She'd felt this before. She knew what the definition landing felt like and she pulled against it anyway.*

**Tana:** "Again—?!"

*The stone completed. East gallery. The stride still said she was going somewhere.*

---

### Chapter Final

*The Queen walked the convergence point. The messengers at the approaches, the guide at the junction — she didn't stop.*

*"Leave the markers."*

*She found Tana at the east gallery.*

*She looked at the posture. She looked at the expression underneath the frustration.*

*"She was recovered once." She noted the difference in the set of the face. "The expression has more in it now. East gallery confirmed."*

*She moved through the combined roster. Brief.*

*She found Lyn. The stance was wider than the early chapters. She was covering both converged groups.*

*"Corridor lamp. Wider base. She lights both groups."*

**Lyn:** "...I tried."

*She found Eirika. The guard position. The weight had settled — the disbelief gone. Something quieter.*

*"Map room. Reading table height. She marks the table."*

**Eirika:** "...is this it?"

*"The meeting point is ours."*

*She left.*

## Ch11 — Debris

---

### Mid-Chapter

*The northern fog quadrant was a remnant of Ch7's deployment — Morrha had moved on, but the Veil didn't fully lift where it had been. The fog sat at the northern edge of the map and didn't advance further. It was already done with the north.*

*The enemy had placed constructs in the fog perimeter: animated figures, former NPCs from the Ch7 sweep, pushed south and positioned as the chapter's frontline flankers. They walked through the fog without difficulty. Player units in the fog decayed fast.*

*At the fog's south edge, a construct was pushed through the boundary. It stepped out of the fog and kept walking south — toward the player's position. It didn't have a face the player could recognize. But it was wearing something that might have been a shop apron. It was the right height for someone who had stood behind a counter.*

*A player unit nearby looked at it.*

*A Gaze Hunter behind the construct fired a suppression round.*

*"Don't get attached. Keep moving."*

---

### Optional Capture

*If any optional named character from Acts 1–2 remained active, the fog or the pursuit found her here. The Gaze Hunters worked at range. Each one was named by whoever was nearest when she fell.*

*The definitions were approximate. Brief. The stone set to whatever posture was available.*

---

### Chapter Final

*The Queen walked the camp. The supply attendant, the kneeling healer, the three soldiers in the cluster.*

*"Leave the camp."*

*She went through the combined roster.*

*(If Eirika:)*

*The guard posture. Harder now — past the midpoint, the disbelief gone.*

*"Collection wing entrance. Doorpost height. She marks what she was trying to protect."*

**Eirika:** "The others—"

*(If Lyn:)*

*The widest base yet.*

*"Collection wing passage. She lights everyone who made it this far."*

**Lyn:** "All of you—"

*She left.*

## Ch12 — The Prophet Was Right

---

### Mid-Chapter

*The chapter's map was exactly what Sophia had shown in the vision. The pilgrims at the entrance stone were exactly where she'd described them. The hermit at the eastern lean-to was in the position she'd indicated.*

*The player group arrived already knowing what was coming. They moved through it anyway.*

*The enemy had been here before the player. Not in force — a forward unit, advance positioning. They had already placed three NPCs in the positions Sophia's vision had showed them in. Placed, not captured: the enemy had named these positions weeks ago and arranged them at leisure.*

*A player unit examined the three frozen figures. They were still in ordinary postures — a seated figure, a standing figure, a figure in mid-gesture. But they had been arranged, not caught. The difference was in the quality of the stillness.*

*Sophia, moving past them, didn't look.*

---

### Sophia

*She was looking to the side — not at the threat, at something specific, to her right — when the Dark Witch's secondary shot found her. A soldier nearby said, watching her fixed gaze: "Reading room. She watches whatever is to the right of whoever sits to read."*

*She had already known the sight line would be there. She had known what she would be watching and from where.*

**Sophia:** "I knew this would—"

*The stone completed. They placed her where the sight line fell over the right shoulder of whoever sat to read. She was watching something that wasn't there. The reader would face the question.*

---

### Chapter Final

*The Queen walked past the pilgrims and the hermit at the entrance stone.*

*"Leave them."*

*She went through the combined roster. Brief.*

*She found Sophia last.*

*She looked at the sight line. She looked at what it pointed toward.*

*"She wasn't looking at us." A pause — not long. "Reading room. She watches the reader's right. This is confirmed."*

*(If Eirika:)*

*The expression arrived and still.*

*"Reception hall. She holds the armrests."*

**Eirika:** "I'm sorry."

*(If Lyn:)*

*The grief and the failed protection both visible.*

*"Corridor lamp. The contradiction is the fixture."*

**Lyn:** "I was trying—"

*"The prophet was right."*

*She left.*

## Ch13 — The Stronghold Below

---

### Mid-Chapter

*Morrha was in the corner when the chapter began. The stone was already at her feet.*

*She had arranged herself there before the battle started. The posture was deliberate: upright, head level, gaze fixed at a specific angle above everyone else's eyeline. Not at the battle. Not at the player group. At something else entirely.*

*By the chapter's midpoint the stone was at her waist. Enemy and player units both fought around her. Neither side engaged her. She was not a combatant anymore — not because anyone had defined her as something else, but because she had defined herself.*

*The aura began expanding before the stone reached her chin. The definition didn't wait for completion.*

*Enemy soldiers within the radius kept fighting. The aura's effects were on the player side only. But even the enemy soldiers gave her corner a wide berth.*

*A Stone Warden passed close. It looked at her for a moment.*

*Then it kept moving.*

---

### Marisa

*A Stone Warden found her in the pre-strike moment — the two-handed hold, relaxed grip, the decision not yet made. A soldier said: "Weapon display. The relaxed hands hold whatever goes in them."*

*She felt the hold become permanent around the open decision.*

**Marisa:** "...mm."

*The stone completed. She didn't seem particularly troubled. Weapon display. The relaxed hands held whatever was placed in them.*

---

### Isadora

*The Dark Witch's Gaze found her in the formal upright — the knight's trained posture, precise. A soldier said: "Lamp post base. The formal upright at the correct height."*

*She felt each line of the form confirm — the posture being named was the posture she was already in, and the world receiving the name meant the posture was now only that.*

**Isadora:** "The order—"

*The stone completed. Lamp post base. The formal upright at the correct height.*

---

### Morrha

*She had chosen her position early in the chapter. The stone came up from her feet while the battle ran around her. A soldier moving past her at the midpoint said something — she didn't react. The stone was already at her waist.*

*By the end it had reached her chin. She was looking at something above everyone's eyeline. She didn't move her gaze for any of it.*

*The stone completed on its own. Whatever she had been looking at, she kept it.*

*The aura from her position expanded into every surface. It did not stop. It would not move. The location was permanent.*

---

### Chapter Final

*The stone was at Morrha's chin when the Queen arrived. The Queen did not look at her.*

*She checked Marisa at the weapon display.*

*"She holds what's placed in her. The display is confirmed."*

*Isadora at the lamp post.*

*"The post has its base."*

*She went through the combined survivors.*

*The stone in the corner completed. The room shifted — Morrha's aura expanding into every surface.*

*"Limited window. Move everything that isn't Morrha."*

*(If Eirika:)*

*She found Eirika last. The guard position. The expression arrived and still. The hands had not changed across the whole campaign.*

*"Reception hall. Final placement. She holds what she was always holding."*

**Eirika:** *[the stone reached her before the word]*

*(If Lyn:)*

*The grief compressed — covering fewer now, the count down.*

*"Stronghold passage lamp. She lights whoever is left."*

**Lyn:** "...keep moving—"

*(If Lilina:)*

*The fury still running. The calculation constant now.*

*"Stronghold approach. Candle holder."*

**Lilina:** "Not finished—"

*She straightened.*

*"She stays where she stands. The aura continues."*

*She signaled the transport team and walked out.*
