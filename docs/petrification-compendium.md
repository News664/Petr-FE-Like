# Inevitable Eternity — Petrification Compendium

> Reference document for all scripted and optional petrification scenes.
> Scene beats are written from the enemy collector's point of view.
> Format: action beats in italics, enemy speech in quotes, statue silence as **...**

**Lore constants:**
- The Gorgon Gaze petrifies living tissue only. The body is flawless stone — every strand of hair, every expression, every gesture preserved exactly.
- Armor, weapons, staves, and bows shatter or crumble on contact. Clothing scorches at the edges.
- The collector never treats the petrified character as a person. "She" becomes "the piece," "this one," "it."
- Physical interaction is normal — leaning close, tilting the chin, tracing features — clinical appreciation.

---

## The Collector's Standard Inspection Format

Each entry follows four beats:
1. **Approach** — noticing the piece for the first time after the petrification
2. **Examination** — close inspection of pose, expression, form
3. **The Silence** — the statue's only response: **...**
4. **Disposition** — where it goes, why

"She doesn't look at me. She looks at something over my shoulder. I don't know what. The expression is not fear. It is not grief. It is a specific, private thing that I am not part of."

*The stone reaches her shoulders. Her hands are still.*

"She chose the posture. She arranged herself — stood straight, hands at her sides, weight centered. She had the time and she used it."

**...**

"She remains where she stands. She is not moved. The stone she was casting from is hers — the base is where she chose to stand. The aura continues. Let it."

## Appendix — Display Notes

### Mounting Postures by Category

| Category | Display notes |
|----------|---------------|
| Aerial (no mount) | Requires ceiling mount or elevated base; minimum 2m clearance |
| Mounted (with horse) | Full floor installation, pedestal; horse + rider as single piece |
| Kneeling/crouching | Low display, eye-level at ~1m; crowding ruins the posture read |
| Full cast (arms extended) | Clear perimeter required; fragment radius counts as part of the piece |
| Mid-stride/sprint | Floor-level mount, directional display; the posture has an implied vector |
| Standard standing | Shelf or pedestal; flexible; default display option |

### The Tears (Neimi note)

Stone tears are the only external detail that survives the Gaze consistently — water and biological fluid crystallize in place. Neimi's tears are the most visible example but the effect is present at smaller scale on several pieces with moisture at the moment of contact. Worth noting during examination.

### Equipment Damage Reference

| Equipment type | Typical fate |
|---------------|-------------|
| Sword blade | Crumbles along the edge back toward the spine; grip often survives |
| Lance/spear | Shatters from tip to grip; haft fragments remain |
| Axe head | Explodes; haft near grip survives |
| Bow | Shatters completely; limbs, string, and arrow all gone |
| Staff (crystal head) | Crystal vaporizes; staff shaft cracks or splinters |
| Tome | Burns from inside out; pages ash, binding fragments; worst with fire-element |
| Shield | Buckles and cracks; metal survives longer than wood but not intact |
| Armor | Scorches and fragments; quality material lasts longer but always fails |
| Clothing | Scorches at edges, tears at seams; survives if natural fiber, not treated leather |
