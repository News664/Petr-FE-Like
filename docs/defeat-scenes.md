## Two Scene Types Per Chapter

### 1. Individual Capture Scenes

Plays when a specific named character's STO-RES or HP reaches 0. Brief. Enemy perspective.

**Structure:**
1. Combat context — what the character was doing, what enemy made the final contact
2. Enemy Brand designation — functional, present-tense, object language. Approximate. The stone sets
   to whatever posture she's in; the Brand can suggest but not specify the final form precisely.
3. Stone sets; equipment crumbles
4. Character's last words (fragment — cut off)
5. Immediate placement by enemy forces

**Enemy Brand vs Queen's Designation:**
- **Enemy Brand** (standard captures): approximate, utilitarian. "She'll go at X. The Y arm works
  for Z." The stone finalizes to whatever posture she's in when STO-RES reaches 0. Cannot bypass
  dragon-blood resistance; cannot define form with precision.
- **Queen's Designation** (personal): instant, form-specific, irreversible. She names the use
  before the stone sets; the stone conforms to the named form exactly. Bypasses STO-RES entirely.
  Dragon-blood characters (Ninian, Fae, Myrrh) are not exempt.

### 2. Chapter Final Scenes

Plays on chapter defeat (all player units petrified or lord captured). The Queen arrives and
inspects the full yield. She sees every captured character as an object from the first glance —
she already knows what each one is before she names it. Equipment already crumbled on capture;
she comments on what remains. Lord is last and receives the most attention.

**Structure:**
1. Setting — NPCs already serving as architecture, pre-placed installations noted
2. Walk-through of new captures — Queen confirms/refines enemy Brand placements, brief per piece
3. Lord — full designation, last words, closing

## NPC Treatment

Every chapter has NPC petrifications. They are noted in the setting blocks and assigned in bulk.
The Queen does not inspect them individually — she notes their structural function and moves on.
They are already architecture before she arrives.
