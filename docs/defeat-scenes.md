# Inevitable Eternity — Chapter Defeat Scenes

> Plays when all player units are petrified or captured.
>
> All characters in this document are female — the Queen, her officers, every named and unnamed
> figure on both sides. No exceptions.
>
> The Queen is the primary inspector in all chapters. Her lieutenants (The Hand, Morrha,
> collectors, curators) are present and act, but the Queen is the one whose judgment closes each
> scene. She has been pursuing these protagonists since the beginning; she is personally present
> for every acquisition.
>
> Format: the Queen walks through the newly captured, physically examines each one — she touches
> the stone, armor and equipment crumble at her handling, the body beneath is uncovered and
> assessed. Then she names what the figure is now. Not what it will be placed as: what it IS.
> The captured have already ceased to be fighters; they are objects from the moment the stone
> sets. The inspection confirms it.
>
> NPCs (villagers, soldiers, bystanders, attendants) are petrified and repurposed in every
> chapter. They are treated as structural material — assigned in bulk to fill architectural gaps.
>
> Action beats in italics. Speech in quotes. Statue response: **...**

### Ch1 — The Stone Tide

*The Queen. Setting: stronghold courtyard, morning. Gate guards frozen at the arch serve as
threshold posts — stone shields crumbled, bodies intact, they fill the posts exactly as they
did alive. Three additional NPC figures from the engagement: a courtyard attendant frozen
mid-carry at the water barrel, her hands still in the lifting grip; a records clerk frozen
outside the archive door with a document in her hands; a runner who was three steps from the
gate. Four significant pieces from the player force.*

---

*She walks the courtyard first.*

*The attendant at the water barrel — hands still in the carry position, body turned toward the
well. The bucket has crumbled; the hands are empty in the right shape.*

"Against the west wall. She marks the water point. The carry posture serves the corner."

*The clerk's document is still in her grip. She takes it from the stone fingers, reads it.*
"She goes beside the archive door. The hands are at writing height. She is already at her
post." *She sets the document back into the stone grip.*

*The runner: the stride long, urgent, committed forward.*

"On the road. The sprint becomes the road marker — she points the direction she was going."

*She turns to the significant pieces.*

*Syrene. Command posture — back straight, tall, broad-shouldered for a flier, the stone with
weight. The sword hand is in the pointing position, the arm extended forward at shoulder height
with absolute authority.*

*She sets a hand on the shoulder plate. The stone armor fractures at the touch — more brittle
than the body beneath, thinner stone. The pauldron breaks in fragments and falls in dust. She
brushes the remainder clear. The body beneath: solid, complete, the strong-shouldered form of
a senior officer, the bearing intact.*

"The arm is pointing at the objective. It has been since the stone set." *She examines the
direction of the extended wrist.* "She is the map pointer. Behind the planning table, the arm
over the maps — the briefing is already using her."

*Vanessa. The dive — no lance, just the grip, fingers still curled around nothing. Mid-descent,
weight thrown completely forward, the body at an angle no standing figure achieves. Lean build,
athletic.*

*The chest armor comes away in pieces under her hands, the stone fragments falling cleanly. The
lean body beneath: the kind of frame that reads as motion even in stone.*

"The grip is overhead. The lamp goes into it." *She looks up at the angle of the extended arms.*
"She is the ceiling fixture. The ascent stopped here; she is the highest thing in this corridor."

*Tana. Small — genuinely small, the lightest build of the group. Armor scorched to fragments at
the pauldrons already. She was mid-step when it took her, the weight shifted forward, the stride
preserved. The expression is frustration.*

*The scorched fragments come away without effort — barely stone, barely there. The form beneath
is slight and exact.*

"The stride has direction. She is the gallery guide — the east hall, the morning light. The
shadow moves with her."

*She stops in front of Eirika and takes her time.*

*Renais royalty. Medium height, well-proportioned — the lord's build, trained for both speed
and presence. The hands are in the guard position, spread, fingers slightly open, holding
nothing at all. The expression is disbelief: not at pain, not at the Gaze itself, but at the
outcome. She expected to still be fighting.*

*She removes the armor methodically. The collar pieces first — they fracture and fall in dust.
Then the pauldrons. Then the chest plate, breaking in stone fragments that scatter across the
courtyard floor. Each piece comes away and falls. The lord's build beneath: medium frame,
proportioned for speed and presence, the form preserved completely. The sword is long since dust.
The hands kept the guard position past the point of the sword's existence. The fingers are still
set for a weapon that isn't there.*

"So this is Renais." *She examines the hands, the guard position intact after everything.* "The
hands are holding the shape of the sword. The grip memory is in the stone — the fingers set for
a weapon that doesn't exist anymore. The hands don't know."

*She steps back to see the whole figure.*

"The disbelief will stay exactly like this. She ran out of options before she ran out of will,
and the face holds both at once — the refusal and the ending." *She looks at the expression.*
"The guard position is the chair rest. The fingers are at armrest height, the wrists in the
holding angle. She is at the center of the reception hall — the petitioner faces a lord who is,
technically, still standing guard."

**Eirika:** *...*

*She looks at the courtyard. The gate guards at the arch. The attendant at the west wall. The
clerk at the archive door, document in hand. The runner on the road. Syrene over the maps.
Vanessa above the entrance. Tana in the east gallery.*

"This stronghold is furnished."

"At the bank edge. The pole marks the water point. She faces the crossing."

*She turns to the significant pieces.*

*The scorched remnants fall away at a touch. The slight form beneath, exact.*

"She was calling someone. The raised arm at signal height is the torch mount now." *She examines

*She removes the armor piece by piece. The collar, the pauldrons, the chest plate — each breaks

"The pivot gives the arm direction." *She examines the line of the extended wrist.* "She is the
map table indicator now. The arm is pointing — it has been since the stone set. The guard hands

**Eirika:** *...*

"The arm at working height. The expression watches whoever reads the map."

*She looks at the road. Natasha with the tray. The gate guard as road post. The merchant at the
counter. The ferrylady at the bank. Tana's raised arm at the approach. Vanessa's grip at the

### Side Ch2A — The Price of a Head Start

*The Queen. Setting: western Elibe village road, pre-dawn. The Gorgon advance froze bystanders

---

*The doorway figures — bystanders in the arrested gesture of whatever they were doing when the

thresholds.*

"The doorways are filled. Leave them as they are — they are the architecture now."

*The children at the fence gate: upright, one on either side, small figures at gate-post height.*

"The gate posts are correct. Leave them."

*The elder in the road: mid-step, the body facing down the village's main approach.*

"She marks the road. The direction she was walking is still the direction."

*She turns to the significant pieces.*

*Farina. The covering dive — body angled to shield whatever was behind her and below. Long-limbed,
athletic, an aerial fighter's build. She was mid-descent when it took her: legs back, arms
slightly out, weight committed downward.*

*The armor crumbles from the torso in pieces, the lean body beneath clean and complete.*

"The dive posture needs ceiling height. She is above the command hall entry now — the body
angled so the dive is directed at whoever passes through below. Everyone who enters passes under
the descent. She is the threshold fixture."

*Priscilla. Horse and rider, full gallop — both stone, both caught at the peak of the stride.
The rider slight and small even on horseback, the posture protective — body curved forward over
the horse's neck.*

*She runs a hand along the horse's stone back: flat, level, broad at the gallop height. The
saddle and tack crumble away at the touch, the surface bare and smooth.*

"The horse's back is the side table. The surface is at correct height and the stone is solid.
She is in the main hall — items placed on the back serve whoever uses the hall. The rider
brackets the surface."

*Rebecca. The draw arm fully extended, the pull-back arm gone with the bowstring. Moderate build,
draw-arm definition visible in the stone.*

*The quiver fragments come away from the shoulder. The form beneath is clean.*

"The extended arm is the coat hook. She is in the outer corridor, east-facing — the arm extends
toward whoever arrives. Items hang from the draw arm."

*Florina. Off-balance — the pegasus fled and she was adjusting, one arm out for stability, body
caught in the correction. Very slight build, the smallest frame among the fliers.*

*The remaining armor pieces fall from the shoulder at a touch.*

"The balance arm at waist height is the grab-rail. She is in the lower corridor — the arm
extends toward whoever passes."

*Nino. Both arms fully extended, fingers spread, the full casting output posture. Young — young
enough that the build is still slight, still resolving. The tome burned from the inside; both
arms reach forward, open.*

*There is little armor to remove — the tome's burn took most of it. She brushes the scorched
fragments clear. The young form beneath, the arms at full extension.*

"Both arms at shoulder height, fingers spread. She is the entry coat rack — the spread fingers
hold whatever comes in. She is the first thing inside the door that anyone interacts with."

*Serra. Staff cracked in half, one piece in each hand, one held higher and one lower — she was
holding both pieces when the stone took her. Mid-laugh: the mouth is open, the expression caught
between amusement and alarm. Soft build, well-fed, the form of someone who was never meant for
this.*

*The bright outer robes crumble in patches where the hem burned. The softer form beneath.*

"The two half-staffs, one high and one low, create a tiered holder. She is the scroll rack
for the dining hall — documents rest between the two extended halves. The open mouth faces the
meal. She is already participating."

*She saves Lyn for last.*

*The Wind Sword is gone. The grip remains, held in the guard position — the hands curled around
absence, the arms slightly raised in the bearing posture of someone protecting whoever is behind
her. Athletic build, a swordswoman's frame — narrow through the hips, strong through the
shoulders. The expression is grief. The specific kind that comes from failing at the one thing
that mattered, with full understanding of the failure.*

*She removes the armor from the shoulders and back, the pieces falling in dust, the
strong-shouldered form clean beneath. She examines the grip hands from both sides.*

"The build is good — strong lines, the kind of form that reads well in stone." *She looks at
the grip position.* "The guard-carry puts the hands at useful height. She is the lamp stand
now — the arms are already the bracket, the lamp goes between the hands."

*She walks around to look at the expression directly.*

"The grief is specific and total — she was protecting whoever was behind her and she knows she
didn't. The lamp illuminates what she lost." *A pause.* "She goes where the people she was
protecting have to pass. The lamp is hers. The grief watches the route."

**Lyn:** *...*

"Main corridor. Lamp stand. The light is hers and the grief is the fixture."

*She looks at the village. The doorway posts. The gate children. The elder on the road. The
six significant pieces placed.*

"Efficient."
