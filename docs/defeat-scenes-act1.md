## Ch1 — The Stone Tide

---

### Mid-Chapter

*The Stone Wardens had moved through the stronghold's interior before the gate ever opened. The stone tide didn't fight — it walked. A servant reaching for a shelf went still with her arm still extended toward it. A runner crossing the courtyard locked mid-step, tray tilted, the momentum stopped but not corrected. An archive clerk at a low desk was caught in the reading lean, head forward.*

*A Warden passed each one with a touch and kept walking. The officer following behind looked briefly at each.*

*"Leave them where they are."*

*She said it the way someone says leave the chairs where they are. By the time the gate opened, the courtyard was already a furnished room.*

---

### Gate Guards

*Both guards were planted at the arch — shields up, lances braced for the breach. They had drilled this stance for years; their feet found the marks without thought. The eastern guard was tracking the approach, the western guard covering the inside lane.*

*A Stone Warden stopped in the middle of the courtyard and addressed the arch without looking at them: "Two posts. East arm closed. West arm reaching."*

*The eastern guard's shield arm kept rising past the block angle, toward the height the definition had named. She tried to bring it down and drive the lance forward. The lance went with the arm — pointed at nothing, locked at post-height, the strike she'd intended evaporating into a held pose. The western guard's reaching arm froze fully extended; the lance fell out of the grip that had no hand left to close around it. The shields crumbled.*

**Eastern guard:** "...my arm won't—"

*The stone completed. Both lances had crumbled with the armor. Two enemy soldiers wedged them against the arch posts. The courtyard had its pillars.*

---

### Syrene

*She had her wing-knights in formation and the lance leveled — the assault wave had broken on the courtyard wall, and she was driving the counter from above the east approach. Her commands were carrying; the formation was holding.*

*A soldier on the perimeter already had a lamp in her pack. She had been waiting since the wing came over — waiting for the arm to settle at marker height, waiting for the moment when the definition would land and the hand would be ready to hold what she'd been carrying.*

*An officer above the east wall said it: "East wall. The arm points the approach. Lamp in the hand."*

*Syrene felt the command gesture not come back. She tried to drive the lance forward — the lance arm kept pointing east, the named direction overriding the angle of the strike. The pegasus pulled into the dive and the rider's arm wouldn't fold to the attack. Every command she tried to give came out as the pointing motion the world had already accepted.*

**Syrene:** "...it won't — my arm—"

*The stone completed. The lance crumbled in the locked grip. The armor surfaced and fell. The soldier crossed the courtyard with the lamp in hand and wedged it into the pointing palm. The east approach had its marker.*

---

### Vanessa

*She had committed to the strike — the dive line set, the javelin reversed for the downward thrust, the pegasus folded for speed. The Aerial Gorgon below her was the target and she had the angle.*

*From above the entry, another Aerial Gorgon said — at the volume of any other instruction — "Entry ceiling. The arms are the fixture mount. Keep the reach."*

*She pulled hard into the turn to break the trap. The arms pulled out instead, widening for the mount that hadn't been placed yet, the javelin slipping from a grip that was opening, not closing. She tried to drive the strike home with the off hand — the off hand opened too, both arms spreading to the span the definition had named. The pegasus answered the rider's body, not the rider's intent.*

**Vanessa:** "That's not — I'm not—"

*The stone completed. The javelin had crumbled before it touched the ground. The armor came off in fragments — not slowly, all at once, the surface claiming her. Two soldiers caught the figure and mounted her above the main entry, the extended arms at the spread the definition had named.*

---

### Tana

*She was at full charge — the lance leveled, the pegasus committed, the corridor opening ahead of her. She had the angle on the breach and the momentum to clear it.*

*A nearby enemy officer — without looking at her — said to a subordinate: "She goes here. Mid-step, east-facing. Leave the expression."*

*She felt the stride before the stone moved. The weight committed forward because committed had been named. She tried to wrench the lance up to strike, to break the charge into anything other than a marker — the lance arm wouldn't fold; the pegasus wouldn't rear. The forward stride became all of her, the strike she'd intended hollowed out by the named pose. She threw her weight against it. Every adjustment confirmed it.*

**Tana:** "Not yet — I'm still—!"

*The stone completed. The lance crumbled. The armor surfaced and fell. A soldier painted a directional marker on the wall beside her. She had become the start of a direction system.*

---

### Chapter Final

*The Queen walked the courtyard without pausing.*

*"Leave them."*

*She passed the gate guards at the arch posts. Their equipment had crumbled. What remained was the stone of the posture.*

*"The arch has its pillars."*

*She looked up at Vanessa above the entry.*

*"Adjust the mount. Face in. The entry has its ceiling fixture."*

*A soldier adjusted the angle.*

*She passed Tana at the junction.*

*"She marks the junction east."*

*She stopped at Eirika. She was still standing — the guard position held, the sword still in the hand, the body fighting the lock for the last span of seconds before the stone closed.*

*She looked at the hands.*

*"Chair rest. Main hall. She holds the seat."*

*The naming landed and Eirika's hands moved to the named height without her — the sword falling out of fingers that were no longer making a fist, the guard becoming the holding. She tried to speak; the jaw was already at the locked angle and the words would not form.*

**Eirika:** *[no sound — the stone had the throat before the breath]*

*The stone completed. The sword crumbled where it fell. The armor surfaced and fell with it. The soldiers carried the figure out. The main hall had its chair rest.*

## Ch2 — The Amber Wake

---

### Mid-Chapter

*The road at dawn. A Gorgon squad moved through the trading post ahead of the main assault — not fighting, just passing through.*

*The merchant at the post entrance was frozen mid-gesture, one hand raised in a half-finished greeting. The ferrylady at the bank was frozen with both hands on the pole. The traveler mid-step on the road was frozen at the step's apex.*

*None of them had been fighting. None of them had been a threat. A Gorgon had simply moved down the road.*

*An enemy officer came through afterward. She looked at the ferrylady.*

*"Pole position. Leave her at the bank."*

*She kept walking. No one who arrived after would know the road had ever been otherwise.*

---

### Natasha

*She had come forward to reach a wounded ally near the gate guard statue — the heal already drawn up in the staff, the words forming on the exhale. She was almost in range when the Gorgon broke through the west approach. She pivoted to put the staff between them.*

*The Gaze caught her mid-cast. The staff shattered. The heal scattered with it.*

*The Hand was walking the road toward her. She did not stop.*

*"Gate-side. The palms face out. Set a lamp across them."*

*Natasha felt both hands opening — the cradle for the broken staff opening past the cradle, the palms turning out and up. She tried to close them, tried to make the gesture for a second cast with whatever magic she had left. The hands kept opening. The exhale that was meant to carry the heal carried nothing.*

**Natasha:** "I'm still — wait, I'm—"

*The stone completed. The robe surfaced and fell. Two soldiers positioned her beside the stone gate guard. A small lamp was laid across the open palms. The gate had its lantern-holder.*

---

### Tana (if captured Ch2)

*She was running the east corridor approach — javelin drawn back, the throw forming, the angle on the enemy archer set. A soldier at the corner said, without looking up from her position: "Approach signal. East wall. The arm marks the way in."*

*She felt the posture claim her — the arm lifting from the throw to the signal height, the javelin gone before the hand could complete the release. She tried to drive the throw with her shoulder. The shoulder went with the arm. The sprint became the standing, the throw became the pointing.*

**Tana:** "My arm — that's not the—"

*The stone completed. The pegasus crumbled out from under her; the armor surfaced and fell. She was placed at the east corridor entrance, the arm indicating the approach.*

---

### Vanessa (if captured Ch2)

*She was banking over the river approach — both arms out for control, the javelin reset for the second pass, the formation tucked beneath her wing. A Gaze Hunter at the far bank said to her handler: "River crossing lamp. The span goes over the water."*

*The banking arms became the span. She felt them pull to the named width — the turn opening into a held reach, the second-pass strike folding into a fixture posture. She tried to draw the javelin arm in. It opened further. The pegasus broke turn under her and there was no rider to bring it back.*

**Vanessa:** "I'm not — that's not—"

*The stone completed. The javelin crumbled mid-fall; the armor came off in fragments. The river crossing had its lamp.*

---

### Syrene (if alive, if captured Ch2)

*She was covering the withdrawal point — lance leveled across the road, the formation falling back behind her. A soldier passing behind her said — not addressing her — "Approach wall. The arm points the way."*

*She tried to swing the lance into the engaging unit. The lance arm angled to the wall direction instead, the leveled cover-position straightening into a pointing one. She drove the pegasus forward to break the line. The forward drive became the alignment of the arm with the named wall.*

**Syrene:** "...it's happening — not again—"

*The stone completed. The lance crumbled. The armor surfaced and fell. The approach had its lamp.*

---

### Chapter Final

*The Queen walked the road. She didn't look at the merchant at the post entrance, the ferrylady at the bank, the traveler mid-step.*

*"Leave the road."*

*She passed Natasha at the gate. The robe had gone. What remained was the posture and the open palms.*

*"The gate has its lantern-holder."*

*She confirmed each capture from this chapter — any who fell here, noted in a word.*

*"Each one is its function. What they wore came off. What they are remains."*

*She stopped at Eirika. The guard posture was still in motion — the sword still in the hand, the body holding the lock at bay for the few seconds remaining.*

*"Map table pointer. Forward hall. She indicates the direction."*

*The sword arm rotated to the pointing height of its own accord. Whatever Eirika had been trying to say arrived as the throat closed.*

**Eirika:** *[no sound — the breath stopped before the word]*

*The sword crumbled out of the hand. The armor surfaced and fell. The Queen was already moving.*

## Side Ch2A — The Price of a Head Start

---

### Mid-Chapter

*The village road at night. The enemy had already been through the western half of it.*

*A woman in a doorframe was going still as the group passed. Still standing in the frame, one hand on the jamb. The light from inside caught her expression: mid-sentence, mid-call to someone upstairs.*

*A cart horse in the yard was frozen mid-step. A dog at the garden gate was frozen in the opening-bark posture, mouth open, silent.*

*A soldier at the far end of the road said to the officer beside her: "Leave the village. We'll come through tomorrow for the stable animals."*

*Lyn kept moving. She didn't look at the woman in the doorframe.*

---

### Priscilla

*She had ridden forward to reach the wounded on the road — the staff already glowing, the heal forming. The horse was finding the lane between the broken cart and the fallen.*

*The Petrification Cavalier came for the horse, not the rider. An officer walking alongside the assault said — without looking up from the map she was reading — "Mount and rider. The flat back is the field table. Keep the rider on."*

*She said it before the Cavalier was even in range. A side document was already in another soldier's hand, ready to be laid across whatever surface the definition produced. The table was spoken before it existed.*

*Priscilla pulled the reins hard to turn. The horse's back was already leveling into the flat that had been named — the spine straightening, the gait stopping. She tried to swing the staff toward the Cavalier; the staff arm wouldn't lift past the riding position. The heal she had been holding folded back into nothing. She was the rider on the table now, and the rider's hands were doing what riders' hands do when they have nothing to do.*

**Priscilla:** "That's not — it's not—"

*The stone completed. The staff crumbled across the horse's stone neck. The rider's robes surfaced and fell. An enemy officer spread the field document across the level stone back and began writing. They wheeled the piece inside. The main hall had its side table.*

---

### Farina

*Above, watching Priscilla's capture, Farina felt the definition land — not the stone, but the word's weight. Mount and rider. The flat back. She felt a brief pull toward something level, something that held things, and shook it off because the definition hadn't named her.*

*She drove into the dive to cover the gap — javelin out, the angle of attack on the Cavalier set. An officer below said, clearly at descent height: "Entry ceiling. The arms frame the door. She lashes to the timbers."*

*She tried to break the dive's angle. The arms pulled out for the framing instead, the javelin slipping from a grip that was opening, not closing. She tried to bring the pegasus around for a second pass. The arms went wider, the span widening to the width that had been named. The dive that had been an attack became the approach of a fixture toward its mount.*

**Farina:** "...I can't — they won't—"

*The stone completed. The javelin had crumbled before it landed; the armor surfaced and fell. Soldiers lashed the door timbers to her outstretched arms. The entry had its ceiling frame.*

---

### Rebecca

*She had a clear shot — the bowstring at full draw, the breath held, the line on the Cavalier's flank. She loosed.*

*The Gaze Hunter found her in the follow-through. The soldier beside the Hunter said, reading off a task: "East corridor junction. The arm is the marker."*

*The draw arm locked out at the named angle — not collapsing back from the shot, holding the held-position instead. She tried to reach for the next arrow. The off-hand wouldn't leave the bow grip. The bow was already going. The arrow that had been hers ran past whatever she'd aimed at and fell short.*

**Rebecca:** "...my arm — I can't—"

*The stone completed. The bow crumbled. The quiver crumbled. They propped her at the east corridor junction, the arm pointing toward the entrance.*

---

### Florina

*Without her pegasus she had been compensating on foot — balance arm out, the lance held wrong because the lance was a riding weapon and she was not riding. A Gorgon caught her in the stumble. A soldier said: "Lower corridor. Grab position."*

*The balance arm moved to the height before the stone finished setting it. She hadn't moved it. She tried to bring the lance to bear; the lance arm answered the grab-height instead, the tip dropping toward the floor as the arm reached for what a person reaches for. The weapon she had been failing to use was now failing in a different way.*

**Florina:** *[nothing — the breath had stopped before the word formed]*

*The lance crumbled. The flight cape surfaced and fell. Lower corridor. The arm at the height a person would reach for.*

---

### Nino

*She put everything into the cast — both arms out, fingers spread, the tome opened to its widest page. The wind magic should have leveled the line in front of her. The cast completed and did nothing — the spell scattered against something she could not see.*

*Then the Gaze Hunter's bolt came and an officer said, flat: "Entry hall. The spread holds coats."*

*Both arms locked in the extension. She tried to close the tome, to start the chant again. The arms wouldn't fold; the fingers held the spread the definition had named. The next cast formed in her mouth and went nowhere because the hands that should have shaped it were holding the air for coats.*

**Nino:** "I can still — I'm still—"

*The stone completed. The tome burned away to nothing. The robe surfaced and fell. They stood her in the entry hall. The spread arms held coats.*

---

### Serra

*She had the staff up — mid-instruction, mid-argument with Rebecca about the lane, the heal already loaded for the next round. The Stone Warden got inside her guard while she was finishing her sentence. The staff split in half. She kept both pieces.*

*A soldier said — not to anyone in particular — "Dining area. Two heights. She holds between them."*

*Both pieces moved to their respective heights because they'd been named at those heights. She tried to reform the cast around the broken staff — the magic was still in her, the words were still in her — but the hands wouldn't come back together. They were already at the two heights the room had asked for. The heal that should have gone to the line went into the air between them and stayed there.*

**Serra:** "This is completely — I haven't—"

*The stone completed. Both pieces of the staff crumbled in the locked hands. The robes surfaced and fell. The dining area had its holder. Two heights.*

---

### Chapter Final — Lord Defeat

*Lyn had been the last one standing. An enemy officer stepped into her path — the one she'd been trying to reach. She looked at Lyn the way someone looks at a sketch she has been carrying for weeks and is finally setting down.*

*She said: "Corridor lamp. The arms bracket the light."*

*Lyn drove at her anyway — the sword reversed, the swing already begun, every line of the strike that had carried her through the village now committed to the cut. The arms didn't close for the strike. They opened, because the definition had them open and the world had received it. The Mani Katti slipped from a grip that was no longer making a grip. She tried to close the off-hand on something. The off-hand spread to the bracket-width the room had asked for. The strike that should have gone home went past the officer's shoulder and met nothing.*

**Lyn:** "...no — not like—"

*The stone completed. The Mani Katti crumbled where it fell. The riding coat surfaced and fell. The arms held what would soon hold the light.*

*The Queen came through an hour later. She walked the village without pausing.*

*"Leave the village."*

*She reached the main hall. She moved through each piece — the entry frame above the door, the side table, the junction marker, the lower corridor grab, the entry coatstand, the dining station. Everything crumbled of equipment. What remained was function.*

*"Each is what it is."*

*She found Lyn. The arms held their bracket. The stone was clean — armor, weapons, all gone.*

*"Main corridor lamp. The arms bracket the light. She lights whoever passes."*

*A moment.*

*"No one will look for her. There is only the lamp."*

*She walked out.*

## Ch3 — West by Firelight

---

### Mid-Chapter

*The village chain ran east along the road. The enemy had used it as the first test of their animated constructs — petrified villagers, repurposed as frontline units.*

*A player unit could see them up close if they fought one. The flour-dust still on the apron. A hand that had been cut recently, the cut preserved in stone. The face recognizable for what it had been.*

*The Construct held no grudge. It didn't understand what it was doing. It pushed against the player units with the same blank weight a door pushes when it's on a spring — mechanism, not malice.*

*When one fell in combat, it left a static figure on the road — collapsed into a new rest position. The enemy didn't retrieve them. They were already used.*

---

### Serra

*She was mid-instruction — staff up, the heal already shaped for the soldier two paces ahead — when the Stone Warden caught her from behind. The staff split. She turned and the Warden was already there. A soldier said: "Junction. Two heights. She holds between them."*

*Both pieces moved to the named heights. She tried to bring them back together for one more cast. The hands stayed at the heights the junction had asked for. The heal she'd been ready to give settled into the air between her hands and stopped.*

**Serra:** "I haven't finished — wait—"

*The stone completed. Both pieces of the staff crumbled. The robe surface fell. The junction had two markers.*

---

### Rebecca

*She had a clean shot at the retreating line — the draw held, the angle set, the line of fire clear all the way to the gap. The breath was at the bottom of the hold.*

*The Gaze Hunter found her wrist in the draw. A soldier said, watching: "East corridor. She marks the junction."*

*The draw arm locked at the named angle — frozen in the hold, the shot suspended in the moment it would have happened. She tried to release the string with whatever wasn't yet stone. The release arrived as nothing. The arrow stayed in the draw the world had now defined as a marker, not a shot.*

**Rebecca:** "...no—"

*The stone completed. The bowstring snapped. The bow crumbled. The quiver crumbled. East corridor edge. The arm marked the junction.*

---

### Neimi

*She was still drawing, still shooting, while crying. The form stayed clean — she had trained the form past the point where grief could break it. The line was on a Gorgon down-road and she was holding the draw long because the wind was wrong.*

*The Gaze Hunter caught her in the draw. A soldier said — noticing the tears — "Writing station. The arm holds correspondence. The tears stay."*

*The draw arm locked at the writing-station height — not where she had aimed, where the room had asked her to point. She tried to bring the bow back to the firing line. The arm wouldn't lower. The tears crystallized at the same moment, because the definition had included them. The shot she'd been holding became the holding of paper that wasn't there.*

**Neimi:** "My arm — why won't it—"

*The stone completed. The bow crumbled. The quiver crumbled. Road-side writing station. The arm held correspondence. The tears were part of it.*

---

### Florina

*She was on foot again — the lance held wrong, the balance arm out, trying to hold the lane with a weapon she could not use the way she had trained to use it. The Standard Gorgon took her in the stumble. A soldier said: "Lower corridor. Grab position."*

*The balance arm moved to the height before the stone finished setting it. She hadn't moved it. She tried to bring the lance forward to push the Gorgon back. The lance arm answered the grab-height instead — the tip falling, the strike collapsing into the reach.*

**Florina:** "Wait — please—"

*The stone completed. The lance crumbled. The flight cape surfaced and fell. Lower corridor. The arm at the height a person would reach for.*

---

### Nino

*Almost out of tome — the last pages thin, both arms still going, the cast formed and shaped overhead. She had been holding the line by output alone. The Dark Witch's secondary shot caught her at maximum extension. An officer said: "Entry approach. Overhead."*

*Both arms locked overhead at the named height. She tried to bring them down to start a final cast with whatever pages were left. The arms wouldn't fold. The wind that had been carrying the spell passed through hands that were no longer hands for casting — they were the overhead the room had asked for.*

**Nino:** "Wait, I — I'm not—"

*The stone completed. The tome burned away. The robe surfaced and fell. Entry approach. Both arms still out.*

---

### Chapter Final

*The Queen walked the crossroads. She didn't comment on the NPC cluster at the junction.*

*"Leave it."*

*She confirmed the pieces. Equipment had crumbled across all of them — weapons, staves, bows, armor. Stone remained.*

*"The junction has two heights. Serra holds between them."*

*"Rebecca marks the east corridor."*

*"The writing station has its arm. The tears are part of it."*

*"The lower corridor has its grab."*

*"The entry approach has its overhead."*

*She found Lyn at the crossroads center. Stone clean.*

*"Junction lamp. She lights both roads."*

**Lyn:** "Get out of here—"

*The stone completed. She was already writing the placement notes. The crossroads had its lamp.*

*The open terrain gave the Aerial Gorgons their range. They flew in two passes.*

*An officer below, watching: "Column asset. Mark the road."*

*The second pass targeted the engagement zone. A supply runner at the field edge. A scout on the rise. Each one named by a different enemy voice from a different angle — the definitions overlapping, until each settled on the version the stone confirmed.*

---

## Ch5 — Last Out

---

### Mid-Chapter

*The border crossing had a civilian queue — a column of people trying to get through before the battle closed it. The Great Gorgons' area Gaze was what ended it.*

*One burst covered six people at once. Not targeted. Area. They went still in a cluster — a grandmother mid-reach to a child, the child mid-reach back, a young woman turned around looking for someone behind her, two travelers close enough to have been talking.*

*A Stone Warden walked through the cluster.*

*"Use the pair with the reach. The doorframe of the crossing gatehouse. Bracket the left post."*

*The two — grandmother and child, arms still toward each other — were lifted and positioned. The crossing gatehouse had its bracket.*

*The others were left where they fell. The column did not resume.*

---

### Amelia

*She had been charging the Great Gorgon at full speed — both hands in the grip, the lance leveled, the weight completely forward, the recruit's overcommitment that her training had not yet smoothed out. The strike was a second away.*

*An officer at the perimeter said: "Corridor approach. She narrows it. Leave the lean."*

**Amelia:** "I'll do — I can still—"

*The stone completed. The lance crumbled. The armor fell away. Main corridor. The lean into the passage.*

---

### Juno

*She was holding formation — wing-folded, lance shouldered, the compact silhouette that the veteran rider's posture asked for. She was about to call the next maneuver when the Dark Witch's Gaze found her. An officer said: "Ceiling. The arms are the signal hub. Run the wires from her wrists."*
